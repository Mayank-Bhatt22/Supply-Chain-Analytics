# Supply-Chain-Analytics
Analyzed supply chain data to identify inefficiencies, optimize logistics, and improve operational performance. Used data analysis and visualization techniques to track key metrics, uncover patterns, and provide actionable insights for better demand forecasting, inventory management, and decision-making.

# 📦 Supply Chain Analytics & EDA

## 📌 Overview
This project performs an in-depth Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a global supply chain dataset of 10,000 shipments. It focuses on delivery performance, disruptions, risk analysis, and cost optimization to generate actionable business insights.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze supply chain performance and delivery reliability  
- Identify key disruption factors affecting shipments  
- Evaluate cost and risk relationships  
- Forecast delays and optimize logistics strategies  

---

## 📊 Dataset
- 10,000 shipment records  
- Global origins and destinations  
- Transport modes: Sea (primary) and Air (express)

## 🔍 Key Analysis Areas

### 🚚 Delivery Performance
- On-Time Delivery Rate: **~87%**  
- High-delay shipments (>3 days): **~8.8%**  
- Majority of deliveries completed within 0–2.5 days delay :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}  

---

### ⚠️ Disruption Analysis
- Major causes:  
  - Port Congestion  
  - Geopolitical Conflicts  
  - Severe Weather  
- Geopolitical conflicts cause the **longest delays**  
- Port congestion is the **most frequent issue** :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}  

---

### 💰 Risk & Cost Insights
- High-risk shipments cost **~54% more** than low-risk  
- Significant cost variation across geopolitical risk levels  
- Unknown disruptions contribute major financial impact :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}  

---

### 📈 Performance & Forecasting
- Two major delivery patterns:
  - Express: <5 days  
  - Standard: 25–35 days  
- Forecast predicts stable delays (~0.95 days) in upcoming months :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

## 📊 Key Insights
- Supply chain is highly dependent on sea transport (~80%)  
- 87% deliveries are on-time, showing strong operational efficiency  
- Geopolitical risks significantly increase both delay and cost  
- Most delays are minor, but rare long delays have high impact  
- Data gaps (unknown disruptions) indicate improvement areas
